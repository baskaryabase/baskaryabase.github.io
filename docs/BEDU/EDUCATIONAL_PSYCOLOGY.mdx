## 1. Meaning & Definition of Psychology

### Meaning

Psychology is the **scientific study of human behaviour and mental processes**. It studies how people **think, feel, learn, and behave** in different situations.

The word *Psychology* is derived from two Greek words:

* **Psyche** ‚Äì Soul / Mind
* **Logos** ‚Äì Study

So, Psychology means **the study of the mind and behaviour**.

### Definitions

1. **William James**:
   *‚ÄúPsychology is the science of mental life, both of its phenomena and their conditions.‚Äù*

2. **Crow and Crow**:
   *‚ÄúPsychology is the study of human behaviour and human relationship.‚Äù*

3. **Woodworth**:
   *‚ÄúPsychology is the scientific study of behaviour and activities of living organisms.‚Äù*

üëâ **Conclusion**: Psychology helps us understand **individual differences, learning abilities, emotions, and behaviour patterns**.


## 2. Educational Psychology ‚Äì Meaning, Scope & Importance

### Meaning of Educational Psychology

Educational Psychology is a **branch of psychology** that deals with the **behaviour of learners in educational situations**. It applies psychological principles to improve **teaching‚Äìlearning processes**.

**Definition**

* **Skinner**:
  *‚ÄúEducational psychology is the science of behaviour of the learner in educational situations.‚Äù*

---

### Scope of Educational Psychology

The scope is very wide and includes:

1. **Growth and Development of Learners**
2. **Individual Differences**
3. **Learning Process and Learning Theories**
4. **Motivation and Attention**
5. **Intelligence, Creativity, and Aptitude**
6. **Personality and Adjustment**
7. **Mental Health and Guidance**
8. **Measurement and Evaluation**

---

### Importance of Educational Psychology

Educational Psychology helps the teacher to:

1. Understand the **nature of learners**
2. Select **appropriate teaching methods**
3. Deal with **slow learners and gifted students**
4. Motivate students effectively
5. Improve **classroom management**
6. Evaluate students properly
7. Make teaching **student-centred**

üëâ **Hence**, Educational Psychology is essential for **effective teaching and learning**.

---

## 3. Relationship between Psychology and Education

Psychology and Education are **closely related and interdependent**.

### Relationship Explained

| Psychology                    | Education                      |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Studies human behaviour       | Applies behaviour principles   |
| Provides theories of learning | Uses theories in teaching      |
| Explains how learning occurs  | Organises learning experiences |

### Key Points

1. Psychology provides **scientific foundation** for education
2. Education applies psychological principles in practice
3. Psychology explains **how students learn**, education decides **what to teach**
4. Effective teaching is not possible without psychology

üëâ **Conclusion**: Psychology is the **base**, education is the **application**.

---

## 4. Role of Educational Psychology in Teaching‚ÄìLearning Process

Educational Psychology plays a vital role in making teaching effective.

### Role in Teaching‚ÄìLearning

1. **Understanding Learners**
   Helps teachers understand students‚Äô abilities, interests, and needs.

2. **Selection of Teaching Methods**
   Helps choose suitable methods like lecture, activity-based learning, etc.

3. **Motivation of Learners**
   Helps teachers motivate students using rewards, reinforcement, and encouragement.

4. **Handling Individual Differences**
   Helps in dealing with slow learners, average learners, and gifted learners.

5. **Classroom Management**
   Helps maintain discipline and create a positive learning environment.

6. **Evaluation and Assessment**
   Helps in preparing tests and measuring learning outcomes.

üëâ **Thus**, Educational Psychology improves **quality of education**.

---

## 5. Methods of Psychology

Psychology uses scientific methods to study behaviour.

---

### (a) Introspection Method

**Meaning**
Introspection means **looking within oneself** to study one‚Äôs own thoughts, feelings, and emotions.

**Features**

* Self-observation
* Subjective method

**Merits**

* Simple and direct
* Useful in studying feelings and emotions

**Demerits**

* Not reliable
* Cannot be used with children

---

### (b) Observation Method

**Meaning**
Observation method studies behaviour by **watching and recording actions** of individuals in natural situations.

**Types**

* Natural observation
* Controlled observation

**Merits**

* Objective method
* Useful for children

**Demerits**

* Time-consuming
* Observer bias possible

---

### (c) Experimental Method

**Meaning**
In this method, behaviour is studied under **controlled conditions** by changing variables.

**Steps**

1. Formulation of hypothesis
2. Experimentation
3. Observation
4. Conclusion

**Merits**

* Scientific and accurate
* Establishes cause-effect relationship

**Demerits**

* Artificial conditions
* Not suitable for all problems

---

### (d) Survey Method

**Meaning**
Survey method collects information from a **large group** using questionnaires or interviews.

**Tools Used**

* Questionnaire
* Interview
* Rating scales

**Merits**

* Covers large population
* Saves time

**Demerits**

* Responses may not be accurate
* Limited depth

## 1. Meaning of Growth and Development

### Growth ‚Äì Meaning

Growth refers to the **physical and quantitative changes** in the body of an individual. It includes increase in **height, weight, size, and strength**.

**Characteristics of Growth**

* Physical in nature
* Measurable
* Limited up to maturity
* External changes

**Definition**

* **Crow and Crow**:
  *‚ÄúGrowth refers to structural and physiological changes.‚Äù*

---

### Development ‚Äì Meaning

Development refers to **overall changes** in an individual including **physical, mental, emotional, social, and moral aspects**.

**Characteristics of Development**

* Both qualitative and quantitative
* Continuous process
* Lifelong
* Internal as well as external changes

**Definition**

* **Hurlock**:
  *‚ÄúDevelopment is a progressive series of changes that occur as a result of maturation and experience.‚Äù*

---

## 2. Difference between Growth and Development

| Growth                | Development                  |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Physical changes only | Overall personality changes  |
| Quantitative          | Qualitative and quantitative |
| Measurable            | Not always measurable        |
| Stops after maturity  | Continues throughout life    |
| Limited scope         | Wide scope                   |

üëâ **Exam Tip**: This table is **very important** for 5 & 10 marks.

---

## 3. Principles of Growth & Development

Human growth and development follow certain universal principles.

### Important Principles

1. **Development is Continuous**
   Development starts from birth and continues till death.

2. **Development Proceeds from General to Specific**
   Example: A child moves arms first, then fingers.

3. **Development Follows a Definite Pattern**

   * Head to toe (Cephalocaudal)
   * Centre to periphery (Proximodistal)

4. **Rate of Development Differs among Individuals**
   Each child grows at his/her own pace.

5. **Development is the Result of Maturation and Learning**
   Both heredity and environment influence development.

6. **Development is Predictable**
   Stages of development occur in a fixed sequence.

üëâ These principles help teachers **understand learners better**.

---

## 4. Stages of Development

Human development is divided into different stages for easy understanding.

---

### (a) Infancy (Birth to 2 years)

**Characteristics**

* Rapid physical growth
* Development of sense organs
* Dependency on parents
* Emotional attachment

**Educational Significance**

* Proper nutrition is essential
* Emotional security is important

---

### (b) Childhood (3 to 12 years)

**Characteristics**

* Steady physical growth
* Development of language
* Curiosity and imagination
* Formation of habits

**Educational Significance**

* Best period for learning
* Activity-based teaching is effective

---

### (c) Adolescence (13 to 18 years)

**Characteristics**

* Rapid physical and emotional changes
* Development of abstract thinking
* Emotional instability
* Desire for independence

**Educational Significance**

* Guidance and counselling are essential
* Moral and emotional support needed

---

### (d) Adulthood (19 years onwards)

**Characteristics**

* Physical growth stabilizes
* Emotional maturity
* Social responsibility
* Career and family life

**Educational Significance**

* Vocational education important
* Lifelong learning continues

---

## 5. Factors Influencing Development

Human development is influenced by many factors. The two major factors are:

---

## (a) Heredity

**Meaning**
Heredity refers to the **genetic traits** passed from parents to children.

**Influence of Heredity**

* Physical characteristics (height, weight)
* Intelligence
* Talents and abilities
* Temperament

**Role in Development**

* Sets the **potential** of an individual
* Cannot be changed

---

## (b) Environment

**Meaning**
Environment includes all **external factors** affecting an individual.

**Types of Environment**

* Home environment
* School environment
* Social environment
* Cultural environment

**Influence of Environment**

* Shapes behaviour
* Develops personality
* Improves or limits potential

---

### Relationship between Heredity and Environment

* Heredity provides **capacity**
* Environment provides **opportunity**
* Development is the result of **interaction between both**

üëâ *‚ÄúHeredity loads the gun, environment pulls the trigger.‚Äù*

## 1. Meaning & Definition of Learning

### Meaning of Learning

Learning is a **process through which behaviour changes** as a result of **experience, practice, or training**. It brings **relatively permanent change** in behaviour.

Learning is not just acquiring knowledge; it includes **skills, habits, attitudes, and values**.

---

### Definitions of Learning

1. **E.L. Thorndike**
   *‚ÄúLearning is the formation of bonds or connections between stimulus and response.‚Äù*

2. **Skinner**
   *‚ÄúLearning is a process of progressive behaviour adaptation.‚Äù*

3. **Gates**
   *‚ÄúLearning is the modification of behaviour through experience.‚Äù*

üëâ **Exam line**: Learning results in **permanent behavioural change**.

---

## 2. Characteristics of Learning

1. Learning is a **continuous process**
2. Learning results in **change in behaviour**
3. Learning is **goal-oriented**
4. Learning is based on **experience and practice**
5. Learning is **universal** (applies to all individuals)
6. Learning is **individualistic** (differs from person to person)
7. Learning is **both conscious and unconscious**

---

## 3. Types of Learning

Learning is classified into **three domains**.

---

### (a) Cognitive Learning

**Meaning**
Cognitive learning relates to **knowledge, thinking, reasoning, memory, and problem-solving**.

**Examples**

* Learning concepts
* Understanding principles
* Solving mathematics problems

**Educational Significance**

* Develops intelligence
* Improves reasoning ability

---

### (b) Affective Learning

**Meaning**
Affective learning deals with **emotions, feelings, attitudes, interests, and values**.

**Examples**

* Developing positive attitude
* Moral values
* Emotional responses

**Educational Significance**

* Character formation
* Emotional stability

---

### (c) Psychomotor Learning

**Meaning**
Psychomotor learning involves **physical skills and muscular coordination**.

**Examples**

* Writing
* Drawing
* Sports skills

**Educational Significance**

* Skill development
* Physical coordination

---

## 4. Theories of Learning

---

## (a) Trial and Error Theory ‚Äì Thorndike

**Proposed by:** E.L. Thorndike

### Meaning

Learning occurs through **repeated attempts**. Wrong responses are eliminated and the correct response is strengthened.

### Experiment (Puzzle Box)

* Cat placed in puzzle box
* Random movements at first
* Gradually learns correct response

### Educational Implications

1. Practice improves learning
2. Motivation is important
3. Learning by doing

---

## (b) Classical Conditioning ‚Äì Pavlov

**Proposed by:** Ivan Pavlov

### Meaning

Learning occurs by **association between stimulus and response**.

### Experiment

* Food ‚Üí Salivation
* Bell + Food ‚Üí Salivation
* Bell alone ‚Üí Salivation

### Educational Implications

1. Habit formation
2. Emotional conditioning
3. Classroom atmosphere influences learning

---

## (c) Operant Conditioning ‚Äì Skinner

**Proposed by:** B.F. Skinner

### Meaning

Learning occurs through **reinforcement** of responses.

### Types of Reinforcement

* Positive reinforcement (reward)
* Negative reinforcement (removal of unpleasant stimulus)

### Educational Implications

1. Rewards motivate learning
2. Immediate feedback is effective
3. Punishment should be avoided

---

## (d) Insight Learning ‚Äì K√∂hler

**Proposed by:** Wolfgang K√∂hler

### Meaning

Learning occurs suddenly through **insight**, not trial and error.

### Experiment (Chimpanzee & Banana)

* Sudden solution
* Understanding relationships

### Educational Implications

1. Encourages problem-solving
2. Understanding is important
3. Rote learning should be avoided

---

## 5. Laws of Learning ‚Äì Thorndike

### Primary Laws

1. **Law of Readiness**
   Learning occurs when learner is mentally and physically ready.

2. **Law of Exercise**
   Practice strengthens learning; disuse weakens learning.

3. **Law of Effect**
   Responses followed by satisfaction are strengthened.

---

### Secondary Laws

4. Law of Multiple Response
5. Law of Set or Attitude
6. Law of Assimilation
7. Law of Associative Shifting

üëâ **Law of Effect = MOST IMPORTANT for exams**

---

## 6. Factors Affecting Learning

### Learner-related Factors

1. Intelligence
2. Motivation
3. Interest
4. Attention
5. Physical and mental health

### Teacher-related Factors

6. Teaching methods
7. Personality of teacher
8. Teacher-student relationship

### Environmental Factors

9. Classroom environment
10. Home environment
11. Teaching aids

---

## 7. Transfer of Learning

### Meaning

Transfer of learning refers to the **application of knowledge or skills learned in one situation to another situation**.

---

### Types of Transfer of Learning

1. **Positive Transfer**
   Previous learning helps new learning
   *Example: Learning Tamil helps learning Telugu*

2. **Negative Transfer**
   Previous learning interferes with new learning
   *Example: Wrong pronunciation habits*

3. **Zero Transfer**
   No effect of previous learning

4. **Near Transfer**
   Similar situations

5. **Far Transfer**
   Different situations
