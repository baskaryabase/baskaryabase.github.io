missionaries

1. C. T. Rhenius​

  • Born: 5 Nov 1790 (Germany) | Died: 5 Jun 1838 (Tirunelveli, India)
  • Called the “Apostle of Tirunelveli.”
  • Served under the Church Missionary Society (CMS).
  • Established 300+ village churches and schools in Tamil Nadu.
  • Translated and distributed the Bible in Tamil.
  • Trained hundreds of Indian catechists and pastors for indigenous church leadership.
  • Left behind one of the strongest Christian communities in South India.

2. William Carey​

  • Born: 17 Aug 1761 (England) | Died: 9 Jun 1834 (Serampore, India)
  • Known as the “Father of Modern Missions.”
  • Co-founded the Serampore Mission (1799) and Serampore College (1818).
  • Established India’s first printing press (1800) for Christian literature.
  • Translated the Bible into 6 major Indian languages and supervised translations into 29 others (total: 35+).
  • Started schools for boys and girls, promoting mass education.
  • Advocated against sati, child marriage, and infanticide, influencing major social reforms.
  • Introduced scientific agriculture and wrote about Indian botany.

3. Dr. Ida Scudder​

  • Born: 9 Dec 1870 (Ranipet, Tamil Nadu) | Died: 23 May 1960 (Vellore, India)

  • Founded Christian Medical College (CMC), Vellore (1900).

  • Started with a tiny dispensary, grew into one of Asia’s largest hospitals:

    • 3,000+ beds
    • 11,000+ staff
    • 2 million patients annually

  • Opened India’s first women’s medical college (1918).

  • Pioneered community-based rural healthcare models.

  • Inspired generations of Indian women doctors and nurses.

4. Graham Staines​

  • Born: 18 Jan 1941 (Queensland, Australia) | Died (martyrdom): 23 Jan 1999 (Odisha, India)
  • Served in India for 34 years among leprosy patients in Odisha.
  • Directed the Leprosy Home and Hospital, Baripada.
  • Translated hymns into local tribal languages.
  • Remembered for his martyrdom with his two sons (1999).
  • Posthumously awarded the Padma Shri (2005) for social service.
  • His wife Gladys Staines continued the mission after his death.

5. Evelyn “Granny” Brand​

  • Born: 1879 (England) | Died: 1974 (India)
  • Missionary to the Servarayan Hills, Tamil Nadu with her husband Jesse Brand (from 1909).
  • After Jesse’s death in 1929, continued 45+ years alone.
  • Built schools, churches, and clinics for tribal villagers.
  • Known for traveling by horseback into her 90s to reach remote villages.
  • Awarded the Kaiser-i-Hind Medal (1946) for public service.
  • Revered as a symbol of perseverance and selfless service.

6. Bartholomaeus Ziegenbalg​

  • Born: 10 Jul 1682 (Germany) | Died: 23 Feb 1719 (Tranquebar, India)
  • First Protestant missionary to India, sent by King of Denmark to Tranquebar (Tamil Nadu) in 1706.
  • Translated the New Testament into Tamil (1714) — the first complete NT in any Indian language.
  • Set up the first Protestant seminary in India to train Indian pastors.
  • Established schools for boys and girls — one of the earliest promoters of women’s education in South India.
  • Introduced the first printing press in Tamil (1712), producing Tamil literature and scriptures.
  • Known for adopting Indian customs and engaging deeply with Tamil culture.
